About Me

Hi, I’m Daniela — an Electrical Engineer who’s always been curious about how things work and even more excited about making them work better. Whether it's tinkering with microcontrollers, building smart devices, or diving into robotics projects, I love bringing ideas to life through hands-on problem solving. Over the years, I’ve worked on everything from PCB design to automation tools, blending creativity with technical skills. I’m someone who enjoys learning new things, collaborating with others, and finding smart solutions that help people. If there’s a challenge to solve, chances are I’m already thinking about how to build it.

I’m originally from Monterrey, and when I’m not diving into an engineering project, you’ll usually find me out on a hiking trail, reading a good book, or improving my tennis skills. I’m a big fan of movies — especially horror — and I love pretending I’m a serious film critic while watching or discussing them (letterboxd reviews pending). In my free time, I like to unwind by playing instruments — mainly violin, piano, or guitar — which keeps my creative side engaged. I thrive on challenges that let me explore new ideas and grow as both an engineer and a creator.

Projects

Capstone Project

BreatheEasy – Digital Inhaler is a smart, ergonomic device that helps users improve their inhaler technique. It uses sensors to monitor air quality, detect shaking, and measure breath strength, then syncs with a mobile app via Bluetooth for real-time feedback. Designed to boost medication effectiveness and user confidence.

KiCad ESP32 C++

DIY IR LED Controller

Designed a infrared remote and reciever system built to reuse leftover LED strips without remotes. This project was started to help save money and reduce waste because why toss perfectly good LED strips. This design uses an ATtiny85 and transistors, it mirrors commercial RGB/W lighting setups and is fully customizable.

Attiny85 C++ Transmitter & Reciever

Testing ROS Navigation Algorithms

A robotics simulation project where different autonomous navigation algorithms were tested using TurtleBot in custom-built Gazebo environments. Focused on comparing SLAM, path planning, and obstacle avoidance performance to evaluate efficiency under varying conditions in a Linux-ROS setup.

ROS OpenCV Gazebo

LabVIEW Resistivity Lab Automator

Designed and programmed a LabVIEW interface to automate data collection in a resistivity lab. The system improved measurement accuracy and streamlined the use of the 4-probe method, enabling better analysis of dielectric material properties in an academic research environment.
